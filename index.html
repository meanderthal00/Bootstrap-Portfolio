My interest in computers and technology started when I was a kid, spending hours in the arcade pumping quarters into furiously flashing machines. Spending time in school marveling at the graphics of “The Oregon Trail” and feeling cool because I could make words run down the screen in MS-DOS. As I got older what fascinated me was how we as humans have the ability to create tools that completely change the ways in which we live, and interact with each other. My education in the values of compromise and perseverance came in the form of my family’s business where I also learned how to defuse difficult situations in a constructive manner.

My attention to art and design was encouraged by my Mom who helped me focus my drive to create, taught me that everything has a process that often requires sustained effort, and that there is beauty in everything … even programming. Having spent the last two decades of my life in the medical field has given me the opportunity to talk to a wide variety of people this has given me the the opportunity to develop the skill of listening and to help people understand their needs. Often times translating a technical language into something that people can understand in a way that allows them to implement these concepts into their everyday life, allowing them to make meaningful changes in their lives.
